# GAMEC Digital Platform Roadmap

*A strategic plan for growing GAMEC's online presence from a community website into a full-service member platform.*

---

## Where We Are Today

GAMEC's website (igamec.org) serves as the public face of the organization — sharing our mission, programs, resources, and donation channels with the global Muslim Eritrean community. The site is fast, mobile-friendly, accessible, and optimized for search engines.

**Current capabilities:**
- 20-page public website covering all programs and services
- Online Quran reader with surah navigation
- Three donation channels (Square, PayPal, Zelle)
- Donation receipt generator for tax documentation
- Makkah and Madinah livestream access
- Matrimonial services information
- Mobile-responsive design for all devices

---

## Phase 1: Community Content Hub

**Purpose:** Keep members informed and engaged between events.

| Capability | Member Benefit |
|---|---|
| Community announcements | Members stay informed about GAMEC news |
| Event calendar | Easy discovery of upcoming programs and gatherings |
| Khutba summaries | Access to Friday sermon highlights for those who missed them |
| Newsletter archive | Catch up on past communications |
| Email/RSS subscriptions | Get updates without visiting the site |
| Site-wide search | Find any resource, article, or program quickly |

**Organizational impact:**
- Reduces reliance on WhatsApp/Facebook for announcements
- Creates a permanent, searchable archive of community communications
- Improves member engagement between in-person events
- Board members can publish content without technical help

---

## Phase 2: Member Accounts & Portal

**Purpose:** Give members a personalized experience and streamline administration.

| Capability | Member Benefit | Admin Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| Member login | Personal dashboard | Know who your members are |
| Membership management | View status, renew online | Automated renewals, less paperwork |
| Event registration | RSVP to events online | Accurate headcounts, planning data |
| Donation history | View past contributions, download receipts | Donor relationship management |
| Member directory (opt-in) | Connect with other members | Community network visibility |
| Role-based access | Board sees admin tools, members see their portal | Controlled information access |

**Organizational impact:**
- Eliminates manual membership tracking (spreadsheets, paper forms)
- Provides accurate membership numbers for grant applications and reporting
- Enables data-driven decisions about programs and events
- Reduces administrative burden on board members and volunteers

---

## Phase 3: Program-Specific Tools

**Purpose:** Give each GAMEC program digital tools tailored to their needs.

### GAMEC Youth
- Event sign-ups for sports, workshops, cultural celebrations
- Photo/video galleries from youth events
- Volunteer hour tracking for students

### GAMEC Sisters
- Workshop registration
- Resource library (educational materials)
- Private discussion space

### GAMEC Seniors
- Service request forms
- Community connection matching
- Health resource directory

### GAMEC Professionals
- Professional directory with industry filters
- Mentorship matching (experienced ↔ new graduates)
- Job/opportunity board

### GAMEC Charity (Relief)
- Campaign pages with progress tracking
- Donor recognition (with permission)
- Impact reports with photos and stories

### Matrimonial Services
- Private, secure profile submission
- Matching facilitation tools for coordinators
- Communication channel between interested parties

**Organizational impact:**
- Each program operates more efficiently with less manual coordination
- Demonstrates organizational capacity to funders and partners
- Creates measurable outcomes for grant reporting
- Attracts new members through useful, program-specific tools

## Phase 4: Donations & Financial Tools

**Purpose:** Make giving easier and provide transparency.

| Capability | Donor Benefit | Admin Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| Recurring donations | Set-and-forget monthly giving | Predictable revenue |
| Campaign pages | Give to specific causes (relief, youth, etc.) | Track campaign performance |
| Automated receipts | Instant tax receipts via email | Zero manual receipt generation |
| Donor dashboard | See giving history, impact | Donor retention insights |
| Zakat calculator | Calculate obligations easily | Increased Zakat contributions |
| Fundraising thermometers | See community progress toward goals | Motivate giving |

**Organizational impact:**
- Increases recurring revenue (predictable budget planning)
- Reduces treasurer workload (automated receipts, reports)
- Enables targeted fundraising for specific programs
- Provides financial transparency that builds donor trust

---

## Phase 5: Communications & Outreach

**Purpose:** Reach members where they are and grow the community.

| Capability | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Email campaigns | Targeted announcements by program, location, or interest |
| SMS notifications | Time-sensitive reminders (events, deadlines) |
| Multi-language support | Arabic and Tigrinya content for broader reach |
| Social media integration | Auto-share announcements to Facebook, Instagram |
| New member onboarding | Automated welcome sequence for new sign-ups |
| Feedback collection | Surveys and polls for community input |

**Organizational impact:**
- Reaches members who don't regularly visit the website
- Grows membership through better outreach
- Gathers community input for program planning
- Supports GAMEC's global reach across language barriers

## Timeline & Priorities

| Phase | Estimated Timeline | Prerequisites |
|---|---|---|
| Phase 1: Content Hub | 2–3 months | Board provides content regularly |
| Phase 2: Member Portal | 3–4 months | Decision on membership tiers/pricing |
| Phase 3: Program Tools | 4–6 months (incremental) | Program leads define their needs |
| Phase 4: Financial Tools | 2–3 months | Payment processor selection |
| Phase 5: Communications | 2–3 months | Member database from Phase 2 |

Phases can overlap. Phase 2 and Phase 4 can run in parallel. Phase 3 is delivered incrementally — one program at a time.

---

## What GAMEC Leadership Needs to Provide

For this roadmap to succeed, the technology side needs decisions and content from leadership:

### Decisions Needed

1. **Membership structure** — What are the tiers? Annual fee? Family vs. individual?
2. **Content ownership** — Who publishes announcements? Who approves content?
3. **Program priorities** — Which program gets digital tools first?
4. **Privacy policies** — What member data is collected? Who can see the directory?
5. **Financial goals** — Target for recurring donations? Campaign goals?
6. **Language priorities** — English first, then Arabic? Tigrinya?

### Ongoing Content Needs

- Regular announcements (at least 2–4 per month)
- Event details (date, time, location, description, RSVP deadline)
- Program updates from each department lead
- Photos from events (with permission)
- Khutba summaries or recordings

## Cost Considerations

| Item | Estimated Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Website hosting (Netlify) | Free tier / $19/mo | Current: free tier is sufficient |
| Authentication (Clerk) | Free up to 10,000 users | More than enough for GAMEC |
| Email service | $0–$20/mo | Free tiers available (Resend, SendGrid) |
| Database hosting | $5–$20/mo | Only needed in Phase 4 |
| Domain renewal | ~$12/year | Already in place |
| Payment processing | Standard fees (2.9% + $0.30) | Already using Square/PayPal |

**Total estimated monthly cost through Phase 3: $0–$40/month**

The platform is designed to grow affordably. Most tools have generous free tiers that cover GAMEC's scale.

## Summary

This roadmap transforms igamec.org from an informational website into a platform that:

- **Keeps members connected** between events
- **Reduces administrative burden** on volunteers and board members
- **Increases donations** through easier giving and transparency
- **Supports every program** with tailored digital tools
- **Grows the community** through better outreach and engagement

Each phase builds on the previous one. Nothing is wasted. The foundation is already in place.
